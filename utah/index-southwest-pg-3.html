Monument Valley

This drive and the views to the red sandstone structures and vast land of the Colorado Plateau is what most of us envision when we think of the Wild West. Lot of Western Movies and movies like Forest Gump, Mission Impossible II , 2001 - A space Odyssey were filmed here. We stopped to fly drone over an Arch by the road side, take picture of the “Mexican Hat”, Forest Gump Point and passed by Valley of Gods.

In Monument Valley, we embarked on a 17 mile drive though some stunning rock formations like Mittens and Merrick Butte, Elephant Butte, Camel Butte. There is no paved road so the journey was quite bumpy and dusty!

Antelope Canyon

Antelope Canyon is a gorgeous place to visit. With its red canyon walls and narrow ,twisting pathways, this place is a joy to photograph. Antelope Canyon is made up of two slot canyons - Upper Antelope and Lower Antelope. We booked tours to visit both.

First we visited Upper antelope in the morning. These are more popular as they have darker walls, falling sands and light beams shining through dusty air. As a result the tour was a bit hurried up esp. toward the end as the light beam came up. It was a great experience nonetheless and the guide helped us take some cool pictures. Lower Antelope Canyons were fun as well, with narrow passageways to squeeze through and ladders to climb.

Lake Powell

In the morning, we decided to take a boat tour on Lake Powell on Ronak’s insistence. It turned out to be a wonderful last minute addition. With crystal clear waters reflecting the canyons on both sides. We saw a couple of kayakers and the captain was very friendly and gave us some interesting tidbits about the lake and its history. It was sad to see how much the lake has receded over the years.

Grand Canyon National Park

On way to Grand Canyon, we stopped at Cameron Trading post for their famous Navajo Tacos. After stopping at the Desert View Point for some photos and ice-cream we went to the hotel which was right on the edge of the South Rim of Grand Canyon. In the evening we took the shuttle to Hermit’s Point and walked along the rim.

The end.
