Road Trip in the Canadian Rockies

4 days
3 treks
1,896 photos taken

In August 2018 we went on a 10day road trip through the Canadian Rockies. It was one of the most scenic road trips we have taken so far right along side Hwy1 in California and Grand Circle Tour of the Utah and Arizona National Parks. Turquoise lakes, soaring cliffs, cascading waterfalls, bubbling mountain streams at every turn made for a truly breathtaking journey. Only damper was on this trip we were impacted by the wildfires in British Columbia, which made for poor air quality and hazy conditions on some days. We made the best of the situation by going on hikes in the canyon on the hazy days as the haze only impacted the distant mountain views.

Denver City Downtown

Day1: Charlotte  ✈  Denver  ✈  Calgary

We had a 7hour layover in the Mile High City Denver, CO so we decided to explore a bit. We hopped on the A-line train to Union Station in downtown Denver. After some hot chocolate and croissants in the impressive train hall we took a guided tour of the State Capitol building. From there we took the free bus ride to 16th Street Mall for some delicious Mexican food at Otra Vaz Cantina.

Day2: Calgary  ✈  Banff

We landed in Calgary in the evening the night before and were shocked to find dark skies and thick smoke in the air. The weather forecast called for perfectly sunny skies and 70/50 degree temperatures throughout our stay so we were stunned. We found out that smoke from the wildfires in the neighboring British Columbia hundreds of miles was impacting the entire province of Alberta as well. At first we got very upset and wondered whether we should cancel our trip and head back home. But after dinned and discussing a bit we decided to go ahead and figure things out as we go. So next morning we headed to Banff.

We had booked 3 nights at the Kananaskis Mountain Lodge and really enjoyed our stay here. After checking in we headed for a pleasant hike in the Johnston Canyon.

Johnston Lake

Carved steeply in the limestone bedrock by thousands of years of water erosion, the dramatic Johnston Canyon is a must visit. As you walk along the trail winding gently through the trees and next to the rushing waters of the creek you can feel yourself being instantly calmed. It is a powerful feeling to stand in the midst of this place, the steep walls of the canyon jutting high above you and the deep green pools of the creek far below.

The catwalks that are affixed to the limestone cliffs allow access to a deep canyon that would otherwise be exclusive to climbers. The trail to the Lower Falls is a fairly flat and smooth. For a close up vantage point we went through a short tunnel to a viewing platform next to the falls.

The trail to the Upper Falls was a little steeper but was well worth the dramatic view of the falls plunging 40 meters into a deep pool below. There are two good viewing spots here and we went up the rocks to the top of the falls.

Lake Louise

Day3: Calgary  ✈  Banff

Today we planned to visit two insta- famous lakes: Lake Louise and Moraine Lake. Lake Louise is situated next to the iconic Chateau Lake Louise hotel. When we reached there the smog made everything look foggy and almost mystical. It was not clear enough to see the mountains surrounding the lake. But we could still see the turquoise green color as we walked along the Shoreline Trail. We enjoyed watching folks canoeing on the lake. After lunch in the lobby café we headed to see Moraine Lake.

Moraine Lake

Moraine Lake is situated in the beautiful valley of the Ten Peaks in Banff National Park. We climbed up the Rockpile Trail next to the parking lot and were transfixed by the view. The intense and vivid shade of turquoise blue color of the lake was unbelievable! When the melt water from the glacier above starts to flow into the lake it brings with it rock flour or silt. Light reflecting off the slit gives the lake the stunning blue color. Later we walked along the Lakeshore trail past the lodge and several colorful canoes. We could not see much of the surrounding mountains but would return later during our trip.

Bow Valley Parkway

Today we set out to explore the scenic Bow Valley Parkway between Banff and Lake Louise. We started the day with a cruise on Lake Minnewanka and then stopped at the Two Jack Lake picnic area. Along the way we came upon a scenic overlook just in time to see a goods train passing by. It appeared like a toy train! Today was not the best day for sightseeing due to the smog in the area so we decided to head back to the hotel and rest. We had s’mores by the campfire and a nice Italian meal.

Bow Lake

Day 5: This was the day which was to be the highlight of our trip – drive along the famous Icefield Parkway between Banff and Jasper national parks. And as if the winds know of our plan the smog cleared up for the day and we enjoyed a spectacular drive with gorgeous weather.

There were dozens of lakes along the way but we waited until we came to the majestic Bow Lake. We could see the mountains and the sky reflected in the mirror like surface of the lake!

Peyto Lake

Next stop: Peyto Lake. A short hike will take you to a scenic overlook. But headed further up the trail to get the best views. The trial was steep and we were out of breath, but were rewarded by one of the most spectacular sight we have ever seen. We spent some time enjoying the view from the rocks and chatting with an Indian couple from Mumbai.

A few minutes later we came upon beatiful Watefowl Lake by the side of the road.We stopped for a few minutes for pictures and taking in the reflection of Mt. Chephren in the water.

Icefield Parkway

As we continued along the parkway, there were jaw-dropping views at every turn. But we resisted the temptation to stop as we had to get to the Columbia Icefield Discovery Center for a Glacier Adventure tour. We hopped on a special bus that can actually drive on a glacier. We took our very first glacier walk! After a brief stop on Glacier Skywalk we were on our way to Jasper.

Jasper

Day 6: In the morning we took the Jasper Skytram and had breakfast at the restaurant at the top. Unfortunately views were not too great due to the smog.

Maligne Canyon

From there we went for a hike in the Maligne Canyon, which is the deepest canyon in the Canadian Rockies and a breathtaking natural wonder. The water that courses through the canyon isn’t just beautiful – it has a secret. Before arriving though the canyons many springs, much of the water has travelled via an underground cave system, all the way from Medicine Lake, located 14kms away.

We started our trail at the 6th bridge and worked our way uphill and then downhill on the way back which was great. There were a series of bridges along the canyon which all offered incredible views. We walked along the side of the fast moving river and it was very tranquil listening to the gurgling water. We stopped to rest along the way and saw many beautiful waterfalls.

Maligne Lake

In the evening we took a very scenic drive to the Maligne Lake. We had booked a cruise here that took us to a tiny “island” called Spirit island. The scenery was spectacular!

Waterfalls

Day 7: Today we headed back to Banff. We changed our plans to stay an extra day in Banff instead of Calgary to see the Lakes one more time with the smog clearing up a bit from our last visit. We drove back on the Icefield Parkway, stopping at two amazing waterfalls – Athabasca Falls and Sunwapta Falls.

The weather got increasingly better as we drove along and when we came upon Waterfowl Lake, we almost did a double take looking at the picture perfect reflections of the mountains and sky in the lake. The whole scene was unreal.

We stopped at Lake Lousie for lunch and were able to get a good view of the surrounding mountains and glacier this time.

Then we drove along the Bow Valley Parkway and reached Moraine Lake around sunset. Watching the 10 peaks and lake in the orange glow of the sunset was a truly mesmerizing and unforgettable experience!

Downtown Banff

Today was the last day of our trip and extra special one as it was Ronak’s 16th birthday. We started our day at Wildflour bakery in downtown Banff for some hot chocolate, coffee and yummy pastries. We reached Calgary by lunch time and had delicious momo’s at Green Chilli Indian restaurant. Back to Calgary airport and end of a memorable trip. We will be back!
